Biography

Wayray thee Lyon is a South African Hip Hop and Afro-Pop artist born and raised in Mabopane Block EW, Pretoria. Emerging from an informal settlement environment, he represents a story of resilience, ambition, and self-belief shaped by real-life experiences in the streets of Pretoria.

Raised by his late grandmother, Sarah Stokie Moeletse, Wayray developed discipline and direction from an early age, with strong values that continue to influence his music and mindset today. His journey reflects a young artist who grew through self-doubt but transformed passion into purpose.

Wayray thee Lyon is building his identity as an artist focused on authentic storytelling, emotional expression, and street-rooted motivation. His music reflects personal growth, struggle, and the drive to rise beyond his environment.

Education
Sello Primary School (later renamed Patric Moloto Primary School)
M.H. Baloyi Secondary School

At M.H. Baloyi Secondary School, Wayray discovered a strong passion for Dramatic Arts and Business Studies. These subjects helped shape his creativity, storytelling ability, and understanding of the music business.

Musical Influences

His sound is influenced by both local and international icons. The 2015 era of South African hip hop played a major role in shaping his artistic direction and ambition.
